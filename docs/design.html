Setting

  • Medieval Fantasy

    There should be multiple races with races having different themes. For example, the dwarves might be more mechanically inclined and have access to early firearms. This also means that there will be elements of magic and monsters. Most countries should exist in a state of medieval technology with castles and other traditional medieval architecture being prominent.

  • Backstory

    Expand on ideas about "Agatha" here.

  • Enemy Invading Empire

    The players are naturally at odds with another invading empire through simply existing. This enemy empire will grow and shrink according to the strength of the players in game. This empire will attempt to take territory from the kingdoms the players are in and will also try to defend the territory it has. This is an idea as an alternative to the notion of player inhabiting kingdoms attacking each other for territory and thereby removing possible content from a portion of players.

  • Titles

    Players can hold titles within kingdoms or the AI will hold these positions. These titles are positions of power within said kingdoms which confer certain duties and abilities. For instance a lord is responsible for maintaing his army and aiding his liege in times of war. This means depending of the current situation in a kingdom and the current holder of titles there might be different quests that could appear. See quests section for more. This system would also involve the concept of Guilds and player owned homes. The idea being a Guild is just a small lord with only a few Buildings to their name instead of a holding. Similarly a player owned home is a Guild owned only by a lineage.

Player

  • Class

    A player can select a primary Class at character creation. This Class should not exclude them from content but rather decide what they are specialized in. More in Classes section.

  • Attributes

    A player would have a few Attributes representing their bodies raw ability like their strength, constitution, and agility. These will likely resemble D&D for those familiar. More in Attributes section.

  • Proficencies

    Proficencies would represent how good a character is at doing a certain action. If you are familiar with games like runescape then this is like the skills of runescape and reflect more accurately how good you are a specific category. More in Proficencies section.

  • Actions/Abilities (Name is WIP)

    These would be the combat actions that a character can take. Each action has its own unique attributes and should not be directly better than the other. Actions however would have counters to each other. More in Combat section.

  • Inventory

    A player would have an inventory available to them which would allow them to store items that they come across. A players inventory would work similarly to a bag of holding. The goal being inventory development is to reduce the amount of work a player must do in the UI for inventory management. This might mean that elements like maximum inventory weights or inventory size will be nonexistant.

  • Hitpoints

    A character has a set of hitpoints which the max of is set by their attributes. The depletion of hitpoints leads towards death which is expanded upon more in the death section.

  • Lineage

    A player should be able to view and trace their lineage. This would give the player the ability to look back on previous lifes and the relations they had with other characters. Some flavor might be added in here with the game selecting an NPC (if not otherwise chosen by the player) to be the partner that each character was with to produce the next character.

Classes

  • Classes should matter

    Your class decision will directly influence your abilities to interact with the world around you.

  • Class decision should not exclude content

    Players should not feel pressure to create multiple accounts for the reason of wanting to experience the full game. This means all content and actions are possible for every class but not encouraged.

  • Has a level and can be increased

    Classes recieve XP when their associated levels are increased.

  • Adventures

    When a player has collected enough xp and is ready to level up they must first complete an adventure before their class level can be increased. This may become something that is every few levels upon testing. More in Adventures section.

Attributes

  • Influenced by Classes

    A mage archetype of class should not be able to easily have a large amount of constitution and thereby become a tank. Similarly with all other types of classes as appropriate.

  • Cannot be directly trained

    Attributes are affected by your class levels and possibly other opportunies seized within Age of Agatha and not through direct training.

Proficencies

  • Reflects ability to perform actions

    Higher values for proficencies should make a character able to perform an action better.

  • Able to be trained

    A proficiency should be able to be increased through using said proficiency.

  • Affected by Classes

    Your class affects how quickly a proficency can be trained aswell as the maximum value you can have for that proficency at your current class level. This means you must first raise your class level to increase the maximum proficency level.

  • Affects Combat

    Proficencies can reflect how good you are at using a specific weapon for example.

  • Affects Production output

    There would be proficencies for things like smithing which would decide how easy it is to produce more powerfull equipment. More in Production and Equipment Sections.

Death

  • Cause

    When a character reaches a hitpoint value that is at or below 0 then they they enter a state of 'near death'. This state continues until they either reach a hitpoint value above zero or reach a negative value equal to their maximum hitpoints (amount may be WIP). Going back above zero hitpoints will allow the character to awake and continue actions. Reaching the appropriate negative value however triggers a death of the character. While in this state they have to make RNG based rolls to avoid taking more damage. This RNG roll might be boosted by certain character properties (WIP).

  • Effects of

    Death causes the player character to lose serveral things including: their inventory, their relations with other NPCS, their held titles, their Attributes, and their Class Levels

      Some items may be retained or retrieved upon respawn however
    • Titles can potentially be retrieved after going through some inheritance logic on who has the best claim to a title.
    • Inventory items may be retrieved if items were insured as Lineage items.
    • Your character in the second life recieves XP multipliers on all Classes, Attributes, and Proficencies until they have returned to their original values.
    • Relations are similarly also easier to rase back up according to how much they liked you in your previous life.

Multiplayer

  • Multiple players should be able to see and interact with each other

  • Guilds

    Guilds should be able to be created by players and have a title created for them. This means ownership of the Guild could be stolen by another player upon their death. Guilds can also purchase land to build their own buildings. Should a guild grow large enough in its owned land it can develop into a Landed title like a baron. More in Buildings section.

  • Parties / Groups

  • Trading should be encouraged between players

    The goal of the trading system is to produce the feeling of some open bazaar market where players can setup stalls showcasing their goods and are calling out to walking by players to peruse their wares.

  • Friends list

    Players should be able to keep track of other players through a friends list to aid messaging them and meeting with them.

AI / NPCS

  • Goals underlying AI design (our own buzzer words)

    Simple
    Fast
    Scalable
    Low Upkeep
    potential challenge

  • Can be used in Combat by player

    To avoid fatigue on the player while performing tasks that should not be as challenging like production skills or leveling then the player should be able to allow the AI system to control their character to perform tasks assigned by the player. The AI should not be as effective at performing tasks which should lead the player to want to perform some actions personally. This should handle itself in a manner similar to games like runescape.

  • NPCS have an affinity with players

    Through interacting with the world you can build up an affinity with an NPC which represents how much the asociated character likes you.

Adventures

  • Should be challenging

    An adventure represents the player attempting to do something which is harder than the difficulty they normally face and overcoming it.

  • Non trivial

    This should not feel like some nuisance encountered every day but rather some milestone of a players development through the game.

  • Solo or takes into account aid recieved

    This should not be something that becomes a walk in the park just because you are being carried by a friend.

Quests

  • Dynamic

    Quests would be generated through events occuring within the world itself. For instance if a new lord rises is the kingdom but another lord does not like him then a new quest becomes available to players should they discover this new quest involving their bad relations with each other.

Production

  • Valuable but not forced

    Production skills should provide players with a good advantage in terms of quality equipment for combat but should not be forced upon players.

  • Able to produce most items

    Players should be able to collect materials from the world around them to produce equipment that they can then use or sell to others. More in Equipment section.

Equipment

  • Creatable

    All equipment should be items that can be produced by players through their production proficencies.

  • Directly influences combat

    The items you attack or defend with should directly impact how your character fights and behaves.

  • Worn / Equipped items custom morphed

    Preferably items should be able to be hammered part by part to produce the shape desired in the case of swords specifically but potentially more depending on how this works out.

Combat

  • Performed through actions

    Actions are performed through pressing the corresponding key. These keys are tied to actions through a action bar containing the keys for 0-9 and left/right mouse buttons giving a total of 12 performable actions from any individual setup of the action bar. These actions can be swapped out for different actions to produce the setup of your desire. Each action has a corresponding proficency(or multiple) tied to it which will recieve XP upon use.

  • Two combat models

    The player can at will switch between having direct control over how his character fights as outlined here or they can opt to have the AI take control of the individual actions while the player only instructs the overall direction. If it helps to think of this in terms of other games then think of the direct control like "mount and blade" control scheme while the AI scheme would resemble "Runescape". There should be direct advantages to the former control scheme due to the AI not being as efficent however it would be desirable for tedious / repetitive actions like skilling or walking long distances.

  • Counters

    There should be an arguement to be made for using every available action due to a system of counters. For instance there might be multiple types of defense actions a player can take each one being better at blocking their own specific attacks. This should not mean that using an action other than its counter will end up in a fail everytime but rather that it reduces the effectiveness of your action if it is not a counter.

  • Physics based damage

    Equipment being morphable by the players means that combat must be calculated real time to estimate relative effectiveness of items compaired to each other. This might be heavily simplified when the AI fights to reduce computational load.

  • Multicombat

    Characters should be able to damage multiple combatants with their actions should it make sense and is appropriate.

  • Partial Quick Time Event System

    The timing of characters actions plays a role in the effectiveness of said action. For instance a braced block might be more effective if given more time to prepare while a parry might be best performed moments before the attack hits.

  • Directional Attack

    Where the character is looking should determine where the character will attack towards. This does not mean mouse movement changes which direction we attack but rather what we might be able to hit with that same motion.

  • Predictable Animations

    Performing the same attack multiple times should give a result that is predictable by the user. For instance an attack labeled "Overhead Strike" should always represent itself as an overhead strike such that the player can plan their attacks on the corresponding key according to the battle situation.

  • Dodging

    True dodging would be performed by moving completely out of the range of the attack or sidestepping but should the attack connect with the player then we will treat their last movement key press as a partial dodge (clamped within a certain amount of time so a movement 2 minutes ago should not affect this). These partial dodges are attempts to reduce the damage of the enemies attack and are affected by factors like how recent was that movement, how fast were we moving, our character properties like agility and weight. Next we would use the direction of movement compaired to the enemy attack vector to determine what kind of dodge we would be attempting. For instance if we are moving away from the sword then we might be attempting to gradually slow the sword down. Altogether dodging should seek to produce some value which we can directly use to assist our block or reduce recoil from damage taken.

  • Blocking

    Blocking would be performed via specific actions on our action bar and would have many forms of blocking actions. All blocking actions however would have a few common principles. A block's effectiveness can be improved through dodging actions, the relative effectiveness of the block action compaired to the attack action, aiming more directly towards the incoming attack, timing, character properties relative to your opponent, and relative equipment strengths.

  • Attacking

    Attacking would be performed via specific actions on our action bar and would have many forms of attacking actions. All attacking actions however would have a few common principles. An attacks effectiveness is effected by the direction we are looking which influences what we are hitting, the relative effectiveness of the attack action compaired to the attack action, relative character properties, the movement of the attacker, and relative equipment levels.

  • Weapon Collisions

    Weapons should be able to directly impact one another this means two attacks might be able to collide and act as blocking actions against one another to decide who would win and take damage. This also means that blocking ranged attacks like arrows using only a sword might be possible if however extremely unlikely

Buildings

  • Exists within the world space

    Preferably buildings should exist within the world space but this might lead to excessive clutter so this is WIP.

  • Should be expensive

    Buildings should be a long term project by their creators.

  • Inheritance

    Buildings are able to be inherited should the owner die. The new owner is potentially one that is not the original player. This system would need tweaking to avoid it happening so often that it would make buildings pointless.

Additional Information

This is an ongoing project and has no hard deadlines. We are a motivated team who love the gaming industry and hope to create something others can enjoy with us. We will make efforts to keep the community informed of our progress and updates but make no promises on how quickly progress will be made or updates given. We appreciate your support.
